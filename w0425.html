rank
n
级别position in organization/army etc
1.
[u]
（尤指较高的）地位，级别
the position, especially a high position, that sb has in a particular organization, society, etc.
She was not used to mixing with people of high social rank.
He rose through the ranks to become managing director.
Within months she was elevated to ministerial rank.
2.
[c]
军衔；军阶；警衔
the position that sb has in the army, navy, police, etc.
He was soon promoted to the rank of captain.
officers of junior/senior rank
a campaign to attract more women into the military ranks
officers, and other ranks(= people who are not officers)
The colonel was stripped of his rank(= was given a lower position, especially as a punishment) .
3.
[pl]
普通士兵
the position of ordinary soldiers rather than officers
He served in the ranks for most of the war.
He rose from the ranks(= from being an ordinary soldier) to become a warrant officer.
质量quality
4.
[sing]
等级；级别
the degree to which sb/sth is of high quality
a painter of the first rank
Britain is no longer in the front rank of world powers.
The findings are arranged in rank order according to performance.
成员members of group
5.
[pl]
（团体或组织的）成员
the members of a particular group or organization
We have a number of international players in our ranks.
At 50, he was forced to join the ranks of the unemployed.
There were serious divisions within the party's own ranks.
行；列line/row
6.
[c]
（警察、士兵等的）队列，行列
a line or row of soldiers, police, etc. standing next to each other
They watched as ranks of marching infantry passed the window.
7.
[c]
排；行；列
a line or row of people or things
massed ranks of spectators
The trees grew in serried ranks(= very closely together) .
The tasks have been ranked in order of difficulty.
She is currently the highest ranked player in the world.
top-ranked players
Voters regularly rank education as being more important than defence.
At the height of her career she ranked second in the world.
The university is ranked number one in the country for engineering.
It certainly doesn't rank as his greatest win.
The restaurant ranks among the finest in town.
This must rank with(= be as good as) the greatest movies ever made.
You just don't rank(= you're not good enough) .
The house was full of the rank smell of urine.
an example of rank stupidity
The winning horse was a rank outsider .
noble
adj
1.
崇高的；品质高尚的
having fine personal qualities that people admire, such as courage, honesty and care for others
a noble leader
noble ideals
He died for a noble cause.
2.
宏伟的；壮丽的
very impressive in size or quality
a noble building
3.
贵族的；高贵的
belonging to a family of high social rank(= belonging to the nobility )
a man of noble birth
n
1.
出身高贵的人；贵族成员
a person who comes from a family of high social rank; a member of the nobility
loyal
adj
1.
~ (to sb/sth)
忠诚的；忠实的
remaining faithful to sb/sth and supporting them or it
a loyal friend/supporter
She has always remained loyal to her political principles.
royal
adj
1.
国王的；女王的；皇家的；王室的
connected with or belonging to the king or queen of a country
the royal family
the royal household
2.
（用于服务于国王或女王或受其赞助的组织名称）

guarantee
n
1.
保证；担保
a firm promise that you will do sth or that sth will happen
to give a guarantee of good behaviour
He gave me a guarantee that it would never happen again.
They are demanding certain guarantees before they sign the treaty.
2.
保修单；保用证书
a written promise given by a company that sth you buy will be replaced or repaired without payment if it goes wrong within a particular period
We provide a 5-year guarantee against rust.
The watch is still under guarantee .
The television comes with a year's guarantee.
a money-back guarantee
3.
起保证作用的事物
something that makes sth else certain to happen
Career success is no guarantee of happiness.
There's no guarantee that she'll come(= she may not come) .
4.
保证金；抵押品
money or sth valuable that you give or promise to a bank, for example, to make sure that you will do what you have promised
We had to offer our house as a guarantee when getting the loan.
5.
担保书
a written promise to pay back money that sb else owes, or do sth that sb else promised to do, if they cannot do it themselves
A close relative, usually a parent, can provide a guarantee for the loan.
v
1.
保证；担保；保障
to promise to do sth; to promise sth will happen
Basic human rights, including freedom of speech, are now guaranteed.
We cannot guarantee (that) our flights will never be delayed.
The ticket will guarantee you free entry.
We guarantee to deliver your goods within a week.
2.
~ sth (against sth)
提供（产品）保修单（免费掉换或修理有问题的产品）
to give a written promise to replace or repair a product free if it goes wrong
This iron is guaranteed for a year against faulty workmanship.
3.
使必然发生；确保
to make sth certain to happen
Tonight's victory guarantees the team's place in the final.
These days getting a degree doesn't guarantee you a job.
4.
~ (that)…
肯定…必然发生
to be certain that sth will happen
You can guarantee (that) the children will start being naughty as soon as they have to go to bed.
5.
承诺对…负法律责任；为…作担保
to agree to be legally responsible for sth or for doing sth, especially for paying back money that sb else owes if they cannot pay it back themselves
to guarantee a bank loan
to guarantee to pay sb's debts
I guarantee that he will appear in court.
If we try to keep it a secret, she's guaranteed to find out.
